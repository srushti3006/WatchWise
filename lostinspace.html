Lost in Space reviews




1] GalaxyGazer	:
Lost in Space blew me away. The visuals are 
stunning—every planet feels alive and dangerous. 
The Robinson family dynamic is the heart of the show, 
and Will’s bond with the Robot had me emotional more 
times than I want to admit. It’s adventurous, smart, 
and surprisingly heartfelt. One of the best sci-fi 
reboots ever.



2]SpaceSnack:
Pretty good, but not perfect. The family moments were
 sweet, and the effects were amazing, but some 
episodes dragged and felt repetitive—always something 
going wrong with the ship or the planet. Still, the 
cast did a great job, especially Judy and Don. Worth 
watching, but not something I’d binge twice.



3]OrbitingCritic
Not really for me. The plot felt too slow and 
predictable, and the emotional scenes were a bit 
overdone. I also wanted deeper sci-fi concepts
 instead of constant survival drama. I get why people
 love it, but I personally found it underwhelming. 
Robot was the best part, though—no contest.



4]StarlightNavigator
Absolutely phenomenal. The show balances family drama, 
sci-fi mystery, and suspense so beautifully. the 
cinematography is gorgeous, and every episode feels 
like a mini movie. Penny’s character development was 
one of my favorites, and the twists kept me engaged. 
An epic, emotional space adventure.



5]TechieVoyager
A really solid series. The science isn’t always realistic, 
but the creativity and suspense make up for it. The 
pacing improves with each season, and Dr. Smith’s 
manipulative chaos adds so much tension. I loved the 
worldbuilding and the idea of humanity struggling to 
survive in unknown galaxies. A great family-friendly 
sci-fi show.
